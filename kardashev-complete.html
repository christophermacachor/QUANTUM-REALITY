◉ BIOGRAPHY

Nikolai Semyonovich Kardashev was born in Moscow to a family of professional revolutionaries involved with the Bolshevik Party. His parents were arrested during the Great Purge of 1937–1938; his father was shot and his mother sent to labor camps. Raised by his aunt (who died during WWII when he was 16), he lived alone in a communal flat from age 16.

He attended Moscow State University, concentrating on radio astronomy, and graduated in 1955. He received his doctorate in physical and mathematical sciences in 1962 under advisor Iosif Shklovsky. In 1967 he joined the Space Research Institute (IKI) of the USSR Academy of Sciences, becoming deputy director in 1977. During the dissolution of the USSR, he became director of the Astro Space Center of the Lebedev Physical Institute.

Beyond SETI, he led the RadioAstron space satellite project for over 30 years, with Spektr-R finally launching in 2011. He co-proposed Very Long Baseline Interferometry (VLBI), replacing conventional radio transmission lines with magnetic tape recordings. He was awarded the USSR State Prize twice (1980, 1988) and the Grote Reber Gold Medal in 2012.

Philosophy:

"The concepts of morality and goodness are universal, like the Pythagorean theorem. Civilizations do not survive if they do not follow these concepts."
