⚡ The Scientists Who Knew De Pretto

Giovanni Schiaparelli
1835–1910 · Astronomer & Senator

Endorsed De Pretto's work with a letter dated 16 June 1903, comparing his ether-based gravity mechanism to Le Sage's theory of ultra-mundane corpuscles. Published as a preface to the 1904 Atti.

Conte Almerigo Da Schio
1844–1911 · Astronomer, Meteorologist, Aviator

Presented De Pretto's paper to the Reale Istituto Veneto on 29 November 1903. Promoter of the first Italian airship "Italia", which flew over Schio on 17 June 1905 — an event De Pretto participated in.

Federico Sacco
1864–1948 · Geologist & Paleontologist

Commemorated De Pretto at the Società Urania on 18 March 1921. On 31 January 1921, informed De Pretto that his colleague in Physics, Majorana, had immediately taken up Lo spirito dell'universo to read.

Quirino Majorana
1871–1957 · Physicist, Royal Polytechnic of Turin

Read De Pretto's 1921 book with great interest because of his own work on gravity. Uncle of the more famous Ettore Majorana.

Gaetano Cantoni
1843–1887 · Professor, Scuola Superiore di Agricoltura di Milano

De Pretto was his assistant. Cantoni praised De Pretto's "laudable cooperation" in the introduction to his 1885 work "L'agricoltura in Italia."

Carlo De Stefani
1851–1924 · Geologist, Istituto Superiore di Firenze

De Pretto wrote to him on 19 August 1903, initially hoping to publish Ipotesi dell'etere in the Bollettino della Società Geologica Italiana.

Alessandro Tornquist
1868–1944 · German Geologist, Königlich Preussische Akademie (Berlin)

De Pretto personally guided him through the Alto Vicentino valleys in 1899, enabling publication of Das Vicentinische Triasgebirge (Stuttgart, 1901).

Leonzio Maddalena
Engineer & Geologist · De Pretto's Disciple

"We have lost in Olinto De Pretto one of the most sympathetic friends... He was surrounded by high consideration for his works." Delivered the commemoration at the Società Geologica Italiana.

Giovanni Dal Piaz
1872–1962 · Professor of Geology, Royal University of Padua

"The disappearance of Dr. De Pretto marks a grave loss for Science. Poor Olinto was one of those solitary scholars whose ranks, unfortunately, diminish every day." — 26 June 1921

Padre G. Alfani
Physicist & Seismologist, Osservatorio Ximeniano di Firenze

"Although I am still in poor health, I have already begun to read that most suggestive volume... I feel that my soul accepts very willingly everything the Illustrious Author writes." — 24 May 1921

Pio Emanuelli
1888–1946 · Vatican Astronomer, Specola Vaticana

Consulted by Silvio De Pretto in 1931 about Olinto's ether theory. Dismissively replied: "What is known of the ether: does it exist or not?... Physicists seem to be of the opinion that the ether does not exist."

Michele Besso
1873–1955 · Engineer, Einstein's Closest Friend & Collaborator

The hypothesized — but unproven — pathway: De Pretto's brother Augusto (railway inspector in Rome) may have given copies of Ipotesi dell'etere to colleagues including Beniamino Besso (Director of Sardinian Railways), with whom young Michele Besso had stayed during his studies.

L. Palazzo
Royal Central Office of Meteorology & Geodynamics, Rome

"I assure you that the things read there made me think and meditate very much. It is always attractive for the human spirit to investigate the intimate mechanism of the universe!" — 23 April 1904

Sem Benelli
1877–1949 · Famous Playwright

Declared himself inspired by the "great Italian Olinto De Pretto" to express universal harmony in his drama Con le stelle (1927).

◉ The Formula — E = mc² in De Pretto's Own Words

From Chapter 3 of Ipotesi dell'etere nella vita dell'universo (1903):

Therefore when we say that matter is inert, we must not understand by this that it is inactive; the word 'inert' explains the true office of matter with respect to the activity of the ether. Matter in fact obeys the action of the ether, utilizes and stores its energies, like the flywheel of a steam engine, which moves by the impulse of steam and stores its energy in the form of living force through inertia. Now if the entire intimate fabric of a body is animated by infinitesimal but extremely rapid movements, perhaps equal to those of the ether, movements to which no particle is exempt, one must conclude that the matter of any body contains within itself a sum of energy represented by the entire mass of the body, moving all united and in block through space, with the same velocity as the individual particles. But such a deduction leads us to unexpected and incredible consequences. A kilogram of matter, launched at the speed of light, would represent a sum of energy such that it cannot even be conceived. The formula mv² gives us the living force... — Olinto De Pretto, Ipotesi dell'etere nella vita dell'universo, 1903
The Calculation

De Pretto calculated: 1 kg × (300,000,000 m/s)² ÷ 8338 = over 10 million billion calories, concluding that this latent energy was equivalent to millions of kilograms of coal.

He derived E = mc² from the classical vis viva (living force) concept — mv² — by asserting that matter's constituent particles vibrate at the speed of light c within the luminiferous ether. While his derivation lacked the relativistic framework Einstein later developed, the formal result is identical.

◈ The Murder — Archival Confirmation

16 March 1921 · Schio, Vicenza

On the evening of 16 March 1921, after leaving the foundry and accompanying his brother Silvio home, Olinto De Pretto was confronted at the gate and shot dead by a woman — the wife of a certain Sandri.

The couple owned land where a lignite deposit had been discovered and partially exploited during the war. They feared they had not been sufficiently compensated by the mining research society of which De Pretto was president.

The murder was for private financial reasons, not political or scientific conspiracy.

⧗ Life Timeline

26 April 1857
Born in Schio, Vicenza — sixth of seven children to architect Pietro De Pretto and Angelica Boschetti.
1879
Earned degree in agronomics from the Superior School of Agriculture in Milan. Became assistant to Professor Gaetano Cantoni.
1885
Praised by Cantoni for "laudable cooperation" in L'agricoltura in Italia.
1888
Co-authored with brother Augusto: L'influenza della crescita e del degrado delle montagne nello sviluppo dei ghiacciai.
1896
Co-founded the Schio Alpinistic Circle with brothers Silvio and Augusto. Published Il degradamento delle montagne e la sua influenza sui ghiacciai in the Bollettino della Società Geologica Italiana.
1899
Published Segni geologici nei dintorni di Schio. Began studying nuclear physics and ether theory.
1903
Published Ipotesi dell'etere nella vita dell'universo — the 62-page paper deriving E = mc². Presented 29 November 1903 to the Reale Istituto Veneto. Endorsed by Giovanni Schiaparelli.
1904
Paper republished in the Atti (Vol. LXIII, Part II, pp. 439–500), February 1904.
1906
Elected to the Accademia dei Lincei. His company became the first to order FIAT omnibuses for the Schio–Rovereto route.
1921
Published Lo spirito dell'universo. Murdered on 16 March 1921 in a financial dispute over a lignite mining concession.

◉ Summary: The Network

De Pretto was not an isolated crank. He was a respected member of the Italian scientific community — a member of the Società Geologica Italiana, connected to the Royal Society of London (via Ernesto Mancini), published in the Reale Istituto Veneto, and in correspondence with astronomers, geologists, physicists, and meteorologists across Italy. His 1903 paper was endorsed by Schiaparelli, presented by Da Schio, and read by Majorana and Alfani. The hypothesis of Einstein encountering his work through the Besso family network remains unproven but is the central claim of historian Umberto Bartocci's research.
