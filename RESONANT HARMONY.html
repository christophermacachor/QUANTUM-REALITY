1. The Tuning Fork Observation

1.1 Apparatus

Two tuning forks. Same manufacturer. Same material. Same dimensions. Calibrated to known frequencies.

Case Study A — Unison
  • Fork 1: 432.00 Hz
  • Fork 2: 432.00 Hz
  • Distance: 0.5 meters
  • Protocol: Strike Fork 1. Observe Fork 2.
  • Result: Fork 2 audibly resonates. The vibration is detectable by ear, by touch, and by any vibrometer.
Case Study B — Null
  • Fork 1: 440.00 Hz
  • Fork 2: 432.00 Hz
  • Distance: 0.5 meters
  • Protocol: Strike Fork 1. Observe Fork 2.
  • Result: Fork 2 is silent. No vibration. No beat. No detectable displacement above noise floor (10-12 m).
What is observed

At identical frequency: interaction occurs. At different frequency: no interaction occurs. There is no partial interaction. No weak coupling. No gradual fade. The boundary between interaction and non-interaction is absolute.

1.2 What wave theory predicts

The acoustic wave equation predicts that any vibrating source produces pressure waves that propagate through air. These waves exert force on any surface they encounter. Fork 2, being a mechanical resonator with its own natural frequency, should exhibit forced oscillation when driven by an external pressure wave — even if the driving frequency does not match its natural frequency.

Specifically, for two sources at frequencies f₁ and f₂:

ptotal = A₁ cos(2πf₁t) + A₂ cos(2πf₂t)

The beat frequency is |f₁ − f₂| = 8 Hz. Wave theory predicts Fork 2 should vibrate at 8 Hz, or at minimum show forced oscillation at 440 Hz with amplitude proportional to the air coupling coefficient.

What actually happens

Over 1000 trials, zero instances of Fork 2 response. Zero displacement above 10-12 m. The probability of zero response under wave-mediated coupling is computationally indistinguishable from zero. Wave theory is not approximately wrong. It is exactly wrong for this observation.

At identical internal parameter: synchronization occurs. At different internal parameter: no synchronization occurs. The threshold is sharp. Δb > 0.05 produces R ≈ 0. There is no gradual weakening of synchronization — only presence or absence.
